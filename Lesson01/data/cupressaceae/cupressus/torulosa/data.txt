Cupressus torulosa, commonly known as the Himalayan cypress or Bhutan cypress,
is a species of cypress tree native to South Asia.[3]

It is a large tree, growing up to 45 m (150 ft) in height.[4]

## Distribution[edit]

Cupressus torulosa is an evergreen conifer tree species is found on limestone
terrain in the western Himalaya from 300-2,800 metres (980-9,190 ft).[3][4]
Information on its distribution further east is conflicting. It may occur in
Sichuan of China and in Vietnam.[3] However, according to Conifers of Vietnam,
only cultivated trees exist in Vietnam,[5] and the Flora of China reports it
from Tibet, but not from Sichuan.[4]
