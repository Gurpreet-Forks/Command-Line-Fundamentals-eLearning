Xanthocyparis vietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese golden cypress,
is a coniferous tree in the cypress family.

## Distribution[edit]

Xanthocyparis vietnamensis is endemic to Vietnam, being native only to the
karst limestone mountains of the Ha Giang Province in northern Vietnam.[2]

## Taxonomy[edit]

It was first discovered by western scientists only in October 1999 and was
shortly thereafter described as a new genus and species, namely Xanthocyparis
vietnamensis, closely related to Nootka cypress (Xanthocyparis nootkatensis),
which was also transferred to this new genus.[3] Subsequently, Little et al.
(2004) pointed out that an earlier genus name Callitropsis had been given to
Nootka cypress,[4] but this name was later abandoned in favour of
Xanthocyparis.[5][6]

## Conservation[edit]

Xanthocyparis vietnamensis is designated as a Endangered[7] species, as only
560 individuals are known to exist, on only 10 km2 of habitat.[2]
