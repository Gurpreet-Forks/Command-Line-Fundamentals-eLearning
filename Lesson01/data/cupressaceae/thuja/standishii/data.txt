Thuja standishii (Japanese thuja; nezuko, kurobe) is a species of thuja, an
evergreen coniferous tree in the cypress family Cupressaceae. It is native to
southern Japan, where it occurs on the islands of Honshu and Shikoku. It is a
medium-sized tree, reaching 20-35 m tall and with a trunk up to 1 m diameter.

The foliage forms in flat sprays with scale-like leaves 2-4 mm long, matte
green above, and with narrow white stomatal bands below. The cones are oval,
yellow-green ripening red-brown, 6-12 mm long and 4-5 mm broad (opening to 8
mm broad), with 6-10 overlapping scales.

It is an important timber tree in Japan, grown in forestry plantations for its
durable, waterproof, attractively scented wood.
