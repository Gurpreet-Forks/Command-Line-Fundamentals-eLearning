Torreya jackii (Chinese: ????; pinyin: feishu; literally: "longleaf torreya")
is a species of conifer in the family Cephalotaxaceae. Common names include
Jack's nutmeg tree,[1] longleaf torreya, Jack torreya,[2] and weeping
torreya.[3] It is endemic to eastern China, in Zhejiang, Fujian, and Jiangxi
provinces.[4]

It can be up to 12 metres (39 ft) tall.[4]

It is threatened by habitat loss and logging.[1]
