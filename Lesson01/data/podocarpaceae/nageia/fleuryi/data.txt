Nageia fleuryi is a species of conifer in the Podocarpaceae family. It is tree
up to 30 metres (98 ft) tall, with pyramidal crown,[2] found in Cambodia,
China (Guangdong, Guangxi, and Yunnan provinces), Laos, Taiwan, and
Vietnam.[3] Its wood is highly valued and used for musical instruments, chop
sticks, fine crafts and household tools.[4]
